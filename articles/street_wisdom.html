What we can learn from a random graffii

December 11, 2019 En Mayordomo

A revolution is not complete without an invertedly spelled love.

Notice how love is spelled in such a way that it is precisely a reflection.

This is an essential characteristic of a revolution. To be able to effectively deliver change through this action, one must look at love at its reflection.

Imagine our habit of looking in the mirror before going somewhere important. We want to look as presentable as we can, but, with ourselves, we cannot see our entirety with the highest accuracy because we can only see in the first-person view. To remedy this, we have to stand in front of a mirror and observe how our images are reflected on it to be able to see flaws that are limited by how our eyes are pre-arranged. With this information, we are now free to make a decision to remove or cover something we determine to be unpleasing. After performing this action, we become more presentable.

This is what I meant by looking at love at its reflection. Our default first-person view puts a boundary to our ability to understand what love - or practically anything - actually is, and prevents us from making the best action possible. One must take a step back and observe and understand what love is from another unexplored perspective in the attempt to at least have a more presentable understanding of it. When love is at least presentable, it becomes more understandable, and therefore, becomes a strong, reliable foundation for ideology. At this point, finally, a revolution consequentially becomes launchable.

Any revolution must be rooted from an intense expression of love, and the full of intensity of expression relies fully on how well one understands the concept.

Graffiti is from some eskenita in Pateros.
